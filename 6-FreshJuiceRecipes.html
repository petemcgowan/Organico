Fresh Juice Recipes

As those of you who read The West Cork People will know I am back on track with daily juicing at the moment and loving it. I don't really follow recipes as such, which sometimes backfires - I'm not sure this mornings concoction of cabbage, celery, kale, apple, pear, green beans, mange tout, green grapes and lime was all that popular with my long-suffering husband for example! Most mornings I use carrots, beetroot, apple and orange, or fennel, celery, apple, kale and parsley. I always try to make my juices primarily from vegetables (always organic of course - you don;t want concentrated chemicals in your juice if you can avoid it!!). A little fruit is nice but mainly I eat my fruit whole, as I'm trying not to get too much fructose (fruit sugar) and juiced fruit would contain a lot of fructose.

Here are a few recipes I have found enjoyable. Bear in mind that you can vary quantities depending on how many people you are juicing for. Each recipes makes about 1 large serving. Most vegetables and fruits I just wash, chop and juice. Obviously you peel all citrus fruits.

Carrot, spinach, beetroot and apples

3 Large carrots
1 medium beetroot
1 apple
4 handfuls of spinach
small piece of ginger

Apple, Beetroot, celery and carrot

4 large carrots
1 apple
2 celery stalks
1 beetroot
small piece of ginger

Cucumber, celery, apple and lime

1 large cucumber
2 stalks celery
1 apple
1 lime

Green Power Juice

4 large stalks kale
3 stalks celery
1 apple
1/2 cucumber
1/2 small bulb fennel
a handful of mangetout
A small handful of parsley
1 lime

Fruity Delights

When you need a treat juice a fresh pineapple - it's so delicious!

1 small melon
